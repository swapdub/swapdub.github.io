People Who Made a Difference

Two people who gave me their time generously and changed the trajectory of my learning.

George Petrou

PSUAS · Computer Vision Team Lead · Penn State, Spring 2020

We met when I joined PSUAS (Penn State Unmanned Aerial Systems) in the spring of 2020 — George was leading the computer vision module. I barely had any programming background at the time. He split every meeting: first half teaching, second half helping us build. We were running image processing on an NVIDIA Tegra K1 development board with dual 4K cameras.

George taught me how compilers worked, how to reason about multi-threaded programs, and — more importantly — how to learn independently. That last part stuck. His guidance in those months is the foundation I've built everything else on.

C++ Computer Vision Multi-threading NVIDIA Tegra K1

Prof. David N. Burrows

Penn State · ASTRO 320: Observational Astronomy Laboratory · Fall 2016

ASTRO 320 is probably the only class I still think about years later. The labs had us controlling telescopes on the roof of Davey Lab — each observation session ran 3–4 hours into the State College winter, with temperatures dropping below 0°C some nights.

Prof. Burrows stayed for every late-night session. He walked us through CCD camera technology, telescope guiding systems, and working with .FITS files from real observations. He turned a technically demanding subject into one I looked forward to every week.

CCD Imaging Telescope Guidance FITS Files Python (IDLE) Observational Astronomy
